## Part A: What All 7 Dives Confirmed — The Verified Foundation

### The Market Opportunity

The EU regulatory wave of 2024–2027 is the single largest forced compliance spend creation event in European tech history. The quantified market:

- **160,000+ EU entities** directly in NIS2 scope across 18 sectors[^1][^2]
- **Germany alone: ~29,500 entities** — Mittelstand at 50+ employees or €10M turnover, effective December 2025[^3][^4]
- **EU NIS2 TAM: ~€1.6B annually** at blended €10K per entity; Germany alone €428M[^5][^1]
- **Global compliance software market: $68.4B in 2026, 14% CAGR** — growing to $116.7B by 2036[^6][^7]
- DORA covers all EU financial entities (credit institutions, investment firms, insurers, payment institutions, crypto-asset service providers) — **~21,000 financial entities** in scope[^8]
- UK Cyber Security and Resilience Bill (Royal Assent expected late 2026) mirrors NIS2 almost exactly — additional market opening[^9][^10][^11]

**Path to €10M ARR:** 690 mid-market customers at €14,500 average, OR 230 enterprise customers at €44,000 average, OR ~10 MSP partners × €100K ACV covering 50–200 clients each. The MSP channel path is the fastest.

### The Three Structural Moats (Unreplicable by US Competitors)

1. **EU data sovereignty — legal fact, not marketing claim.** The CLOUD Act applies to US-incorporated companies regardless of data storage location[^20][^21][^22]. Kerno as an EU-incorporated entity with no US presence cannot be served a CLOUD Act warrant. This is verifiable in due diligence. Vanta ($4.15B valuation[^23]) and Drata ($2B valuation[^24]) are permanently exposed.

2. **Member-state-specific regulatory intelligence.** Germany's NIS2UmsuCG, France's ANSSI Cyber France Reference Framework, Netherlands DNB/AFM enforcement posture — 22 EU states have divergent implementation details[^25][^26][^27]. Vanta ships one generic NIS2 overlay. Kerno ships country-specific evidence formats, deadline calendars, and authority submission formats. Non-replicable within 2–3 years of competitor investment.

3. **Accumulated compliance intelligence as data moat.** Override decisions, confidence scores, recommendation history, and evidence links accumulate as tenant-specific institutional memory[^28][^29]. EU Data Act (effective September 12, 2025) requires all SaaS vendors to enable data portability[^30][^31] — but a Kerno export showing 36 months of override history and DORA Register builds is functionally non-migratable without Kerno's models. The moat grows with tenure.

## Part D: The Verified Pricing Architecture

### Hybrid Model (Sprint 1 Decision — Cannot Be Retrofitted Easily)

| Tier | Component | Pricing | Driver |
|---|---|---|---|
| Base | Subscription per entity/tenant | Fixed annual fee by segment | Floor revenue |
| Expansion | Per-regulation module add-on | +€X per framework activated | Every new regulation = upsell |
| Usage | Connector activations, evidence records, AI credits | Variable | Natural growth upsell |
| Operator | MSP flat seat + per-managed-entity | Separate SKU | Channel multiplier |

**Why this matters:** Flat pricing caps NRR at ~100% minus churn. Hybrid pricing achieves 108–118% NRR[^50][^51]. At 11x EV/Revenue, a 10-point NRR improvement = 20–30% valuation uplift[^15]. This decision made in Sprint 1 is worth tens of millions at exit.

## Part F: Sprint Roadmap — Verified, Deadline-Anchored

### Build Philosophy: MVP First, Platform Second

The hard deadline forcing MVP urgency is **September 11, 2026** (CRA reporting goes live — 12 weeks from now). Before that date, Kerno needs a working DORA incident workflow AND a CRA vulnerability reporting stub. These two features, combined with the NIS2 control library from Docs 11–13, constitute a legitimate MVP that generates real design partner value.

**Assume: 2-week sprints, 2-person engineering team (expandable)**

***

### Sprint 1 (Weeks 1–2) — Foundation Lock-In
**Status: In progress (Docs 1–13 being built)**

Goals:
- Complete Docs 1–13 (core data, recommendation, and evidence architecture)
- Lock in hybrid pricing contract structure (cannot retrofit)
- Formalise EU entity incorporation if not done — CLOUD Act moat requires this from Day 1
- Set up EU-hosted infrastructure: AWS Frankfurt, EU KMS, EU-region AI inference (Mistral or Bedrock EU)
- Initiate Diligent relationship (cold outreach to Head of Corporate Development)

Deliverable: Running codebase with tenant isolation, NIS2 control library, DORA evidence links, and explainable recommendation engine

***

### Sprint 2 (Weeks 3–4) — DORA Register of Information (Live Feature)
**Urgency: Annual cycle March 31, 2027; BaFin window March 9–30, 2027**

Goals:
- `src/services/roi_service.py` — DORA Register of Information as a **continuously maintained live register** (not annual export)
- 15 EBA xBRL-CSV templates (RT.01.01 through RT.06.01) pre-populated from vendor register
- Real-time update triggers: new vendor contract added → register updated automatically
- 116 ESA quality check validator (inline pre-submission; addresses the 93.5% first-cycle failure rate[^52])
- Criticality reclassification workflow
- "Add Kerno as your ICT third-party" self-service onboarding widget (auto-populates customer's RoI with Kerno's own LEI and service details)
- BaFin March 9–30 window and DNB March 2 window pre-configured in authority calendar

**EU Data Act compliance (mandatory by January 2027[^30]):**
- Standard contract terms with 2-month termination right
- Portability API (export all evidence records, recommendations, override history in structured JSON)

Deliverable: DORA RoI live register with xBRL-CSV export and 116-check validator. First design partner demo-able.

***

### Sprint 3 (Weeks 5–6) — DORA Incident Management + CRA Stub
**Hard deadline: September 11, 2026 — CRA reporting goes live in ~12 weeks from project start**

Goals:
- DORA incident classification module (Art. 18 major/non-major criteria)
- 24h initial notification + 72h intermediate + 1-month final report workflow
- Multi-jurisdiction simultaneous routing: BaFin + DNB + ANSSI in single incident (addresses 33% cross-border incident rate[^53])
- **CRA vulnerability reporting stub:**
  - 24h early warning to national CSIRT + ENISA
  - 72h full notification
  - 14-day final report (Art. 14 format[^54][^55])
  - CVD policy generator
  - CRA SRP API integration placeholder (API not yet final[^56] — build to Art. 14 requirements; add SRP direct submission when API finalises pre-September 2026)

Deliverable: Working DORA incident lifecycle + CRA reporting stub. This is the **MVP gate** — these two features + NIS2 from Sprint 1 constitute a marketable product before September 2026.

***

### Sprint 4 (Weeks 7–8) — Germany/BSI Member-State Module
**Why now: 29,500 entities in scope; BSI registration passed; audit demand imminent[^3][^4]**

Goals:
- BSI-specific evidence format templates (BSIG §30 risk management, §31 incident reporting, §32 supply chain)
- NIS2UmsuCG scope calculator (are you essential or important? — auto-classifies based on employee count, turnover, sector)
- BSI portal registration documentation generator
- Management liability evidence package (BSIG §38 — board members personally liable; generates the documentation that protects management)
- BaFin DORA documentation checklist as pre-built evidence template[^57]
- **DORA TLPT supplier evidence pack** (Delegated Regulation 2025/1190[^58]): generates the ICT supplier documentation financial entity TLPT planners need from Kerno
- DORA Concentration Risk / CTPP Dashboard: maps customer ICT vendor register against 19 CTPP designations (AWS, Google Cloud, Microsoft, SAP, Oracle)[^47]; flags concentration risk; generates exit strategy templates

Deliverable: Germany is Kerno's first country-specific module. This is the sales tool for the 29,500-entity German market. Onboard first 5–10 paying German customers.

***

### Sprint 5 (Weeks 9–10) — Netherlands/France + MSP Operator Tier
**Why now: DNB issued first enforcement letters[^32]; France NIS2 law expected mid-2026[^26]**

Goals:
- Netherlands/DNB module: MyDNB submission format; Excel-to-xBRL-CSV; DNB risk-based oversight checklist; AFM sector overlays
- France/ANSSI module: Cyber France Reference Framework (March 2026)[^26]; ANSSI pre-registration format; sector allocation guide
- **MSP Operator tier:**
  - Multi-tenant operator dashboard (existing RLS architecture supports this natively)
  - Per-managed-entity billing abstraction
  - Managed entity onboarding workflow (MSP adds client → client gets isolated tenant → pre-populated with MSP-selected control library)
  - Partner portal v1 (Big 4 and boutique advisor onboarding; lead protection; co-marketing pack)
- Regnology partnership approach: integration spec for Kerno DORA RoI → Regnology ABACUS reporting pipeline
- **Zero-retention pass-through Jira connector upgrade:**
  - Bi-directional sync: Kerno recommendations → Jira remediation tickets
  - JSM incident trigger → DORA incident classification in Kerno
  - Zero-data-retention architecture documentation (NIS2 Art. 21(2)(d) supply chain procurement requirement[^59])

Deliverable: Two more country modules. MSP operator tier live. First Big 4 partner on-boarded. 20–30 paying customers.

***

### Sprint 6 (Weeks 11–12) — CRA SRP API + CMDB Connector + ISO 27001 Crosswalk
**Hard deadline: September 11, 2026 — CRA SRP goes live**

Goals:
- CRA SRP direct submission API integration (API expected to finalise by August 2026[^56][^60])
- SBOM (Software Bill of Materials) generator integration for CRA Art. 13 (links to Keysight/SBOM tooling or generates basic SBOM from CMDB data)
- CMDB connector enhancements: pull ICT asset inventory for DORA Art. 5 ICT asset management; feeds RoI template RT.01.01
- ISO 27001:2022 crosswalk: map all NIS2 Art. 21 controls to ISO 27001:2022 clauses (ENISA guidance does this mapping[^61][^62] — implement as an automated crosswalk report)

Deliverable: Full CRA Sprint 3 stub converted to live product. ISO 27001 crosswalk makes Kerno relevant to ISO 27001 buyers (expanding TAM beyond NIS2-only).

**This is the end of MVP. By Sprint 6 (week 12), Kerno has: NIS2 + DORA RoI + DORA Incidents + CRA reporting + Germany/BSI + Netherlands/DNB + France/ANSSI + MSP operator tier + Big 4 partner channel. This is a demonstrable, paying product.**

***

### Sprint 7 (Weeks 13–14) — AI Act / ISO 42001 Module
**Sell as: "ISO 42001 evidence generation" — 40% of enterprise procurement now requires it[^63]**

Goals:
- ISO 42001 control library (AI lifecycle management, risk evidence, model documentation, drift monitoring)
- AI system inventory (register of AI systems in use — maps to Art. 49 EU AI database[^35])
- Art. 50 transparency documentation generator (watermarking evidence, human oversight records)
- ISO 42001 gap assessment report (generates the audit-ready evidence pack)
- AI Act Art. 5 prohibited practice self-assessment checklist

Deliverable: First AI governance module. Sell to enterprise procurement teams requiring ISO 42001 today, not to AI Act deadline compliance (which is Dec 2027[^36]).

***

### Sprint 8 (Weeks 15–16) — Live Regulatory Intelligence Feed
**This is the feature that transforms Kerno from static compliance to intelligence platform**

Goals:
- Regulatory change monitoring: ENISA, EUR-Lex, BaFin, ANSSI, DNB, ICO (UK) — automated ingestion of regulatory updates
- Impact assessment engine: when a new implementing act or guidance is published, Kerno auto-flags which controls are affected for each tenant
- Control library auto-update notifications (not auto-update — human approval required; but auto-flag)
- NIS2 Commission Amendment monitoring (January 2026 package in progress[^1])

Deliverable: This feature alone justifies a pricing tier uplift. "Kerno monitors regulatory change so your compliance team doesn't have to" is the conversion message that closes enterprise deals.

***

### Sprint 9 (Weeks 17–18) — UK CS&R Bill Module
**Why: Royal Assent expected by sprint start; first movers capture UK MSP segment**

Goals:
- UK CS&R Bill control library (90% overlap with NIS2 — primarily a mapping exercise[^64][^10])

- UK ICO NIS incident reporting integration
- UK-specific scope calculator (OES, RDS, MSP thresholds)

Deliverable: UK module. Opens a second market with zero additional architecture work. Kerno is the only EU-native platform with a UK module — no UK-native tool will have the EU depth and no EU tool will ship UK first.

### Sprint 10 (Weeks 19–20) — Growth Infrastructure + Partner Portal v2
**ARR target by Sprint 10: €2–3M (30–50 direct + 3–5 MSP partners)**

Goals:
- Partner portal v2: self-service partner onboarding, deal registration, co-branded reports

- Pricing model implementation: hybrid subscription + module + usage billing

Deliverable: Full growth engine in place. Partner channel generating deals. This is the inflection point where word-of-mouth and partner referrals begin to reduce direct CAC.

## Part G: MVP Definition — Do Not Overshoot

**Minimum Viable Product = End of Sprint 6 (Week 12)**

A Kerno MVP that generates real customer value and revenue has exactly these features:

1. ✅ NIS2 control library with evidence linking and explainable recommendations (Docs 11–13)
2. ✅ DORA Register of Information (live register, xBRL-CSV export, 116-check validator)
3. ✅ DORA Incident Management (24h/72h/1-month workflow, multi-jurisdiction routing)
4. ✅ CRA vulnerability reporting (Art. 14 format, SRP API integration)
5. ✅ Germany/BSI member-state module (29,500 entity market)
6. ✅ Netherlands/DNB module (first enforcement actions live)
7. ✅ France/ANSSI module (law expected mid-2026)
8. ✅ MSP operator tier (multi-tenant, per-managed-entity billing)
9. ✅ EU data sovereignty architecture (EU KMS, EU AI inference, zero CLOUD Act exposure)
10. ✅ Zero-retention Jira connector (bi-directional, NIS2 supply chain compliant)

**What is NOT in the MVP (and should not be):**
- AI Act full module (Sprint 7 — not needed until Dec 2027 for Annex III)

- UK module (Sprint 9 — Royal Assent timing uncertain)
- CSRD module (Sprint 8+ — Omnibus I narrowed scope severely[^40][^41])
- ISO 27001 full audit automation (Sprint 6 crosswalk is enough for MVP)
- Multi-language UI (Sprint 8+)

## Part H: What Happens Next — Recommended Actions in Order

### Immediately (This Week)

1. **Lock in hybrid pricing architecture in the contract template.** This cannot be retrofitted. Every design partner agreement should include module expansion pricing and a usage component. One week to draft.

2. **Confirm EU entity status.** If Kerno is not yet incorporated in an EU member state, this must happen before any customer conversation. The CLOUD Act moat claim is a legal fact only if the entity is genuinely EU-incorporated with no US presence.

3. **Start Diligent outreach.** Three acquisitions in 12 months, EU-native focus, NIS2/DORA gap[^67][^68][^45]. A warm relationship started now converts to a term sheet within 18 months. Target: Head of Corporate Development at Diligent.

4. **Begin Claude prompt series for Docs 14–20.** The codebase through Doc 13 covers the core recommendation engine. Docs 14+ should cover: DORA RoI service (Sprint 2), incident service (Sprint 3), CRA reporting service (Sprint 3), country-specific modules (Sprint 4–5), MSP operator tier (Sprint 5), and the regulatory intelligence feed (Sprint 8). Each doc follows the same prompt pattern established in Docs 10–13.

### Sprint 1 (Weeks 1–2)

5. **Sign 3–5 design partners.** Target: German financial entities or Mittelstand companies that registered with BSI in March 2026 and have done nothing since. These are the warmest possible leads — they have a documented legal obligation, a missed soft deadline, and no solution. Offer 12 months free in exchange for weekly feedback sessions.

6. **Set up regulatory monitoring.** Subscribe to ENISA, EUR-Lex alerts, BaFin Meldungen, DNB supervisory news, ANSSI publications. Assign one team member to a weekly 30-minute regulatory digest. This is the manual precursor to Sprint 8's automated intelligence feed.

### Sprint 2–3 (Weeks 3–6)

7. **Build DORA RoI first, not NIS2 reporting.** The DORA RoI xBRL-CSV with 116 quality checks is the most differentiating near-term feature — no pure-play compliance automation competitor has shipped this, the pain is acute (93.5% failure rate in first cycle[^52]), and the March 2027 annual cycle creates a recurring forcing event.

8. **Approach Regnology partner programme.** Their financial entity customers need exactly what Kerno builds. Their existing Big 4 partner ecosystem (Deloitte, KPMG, PwC, d-fine[^69][^70]) is the distribution channel for Kerno's financial services GTM. Target: Regnology's Head of Alliances.

### Before September 11, 2026 (Sprint 6 Gate)

9. **Ship CRA SRP integration.** The ENISA Single Reporting Platform API testing phase is open now[^56][^71]. Register for beta access immediately — teams that participate get a process advantage before the September 11 hard deadline[^60].

10. **First paying customers.** Sprint 6 is the MVP gate. By September 11, 2026, Kerno should have 5–10 paying customers generating €50K–€150K ARR. This is not a financial target — it is a validation gate. Without paying customers by Sprint 6, re-evaluate product-market fit before Sprint 7.
